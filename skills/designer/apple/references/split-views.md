## Split Views

A split view manages the presentation of multiple adjacent panes of content, each of which can contain a variety of components — tables, collections, images, and custom views.

A split view is typically used to show multiple levels of an app's hierarchy at once and support navigation between them. Selecting an item in the primary pane displays its contents in the secondary pane, and a tertiary pane can appear when secondary items hold additional content.

A common pattern is to use a split view as a sidebar-driven layout: the leading pane lists top-level collections, with secondary and optional tertiary panes showing child collections and item detail. Less commonly, a split view can host supplementary groups of functionality alongside a primary view — for example, Keynote on macOS surrounds the slide canvas with panes for the slide navigator, presenter notes, and inspector.

## Best Practices

**Persistently highlight the current selection in each pane that leads to the detail view.** A persistent selection clarifies the relationship between panes and helps people stay oriented as they navigate.

**Consider letting people drag and drop content between panes.** Because a split view exposes multiple levels of hierarchy at once, dragging between panes is a natural way to move content across parts of the app.
